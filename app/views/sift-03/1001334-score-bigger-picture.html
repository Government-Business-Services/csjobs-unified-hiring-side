1001334

Policy Adviser

Behaviour Seeing the Big Picture

Understand the strategic drivers for your area of work. Align activities to contribute to wider organisational priorities. Remain alert to emerging issues and trends which might impact your work area. Seek out and share experiences to develop knowledge of the team’s business area. Understand how the strategies and activities of the team create value and meet the diverse needs of all stakeholders.

{% set scoreHTML %}

1 - Not demonstrated

No positive evidence and/or substantial negative evidence demonstrated.

2 - Minimal demonstration

Limited positive evidence and/or mainly negative evidence demonstrated.

3 - Moderate demonstration

Moderate positive evidence but some negative evidence demonstrated.

4 - Acceptable demonstration

Adequate positive evidence and any negative evidence would not cause concern.

5 - Good demonstration

Substantial positive evidence of the behaviour.

6 - Strong demonstration

Substantial positive evidence; includes some evidence of exceeding expectations at this level.

7 - Outstanding demonstration

The evidence provided wholly exceeds expectation at this level.

Candidate 1

When it came to tackling the issue at hand, I knew that seeing the big picture was my secret weapon. I’ve always had a knack for zooming out and taking in the landscape. This allowed me to identify the root cause that was fuelling problems, and then I collaborated with the right people to make my vision come to life.

It was a thrilling experience that made a real impact when it was needed.

Before I could rally my team to contribute to the solution, I had to get to the bottom of what was driving the policy. It was crucial for me to understand the main drivers so that I could align our activities with the department’s priorities. It was like putting together a puzzle, and once I had all the pieces, I could create a clear roadmap for my team to follow. It was a challenge, but I was determined to make it work.

I had to inspire my team to persevere with a challenging objective, empowering them to take ownership of their work. I ensured that each member of my team felt valued and appreciated, instilling in them a sense of pride and ownership in their work. Through my leadership, every individual was encouraged to reach their full potential and contribute to the collective success of the team.

In the end, my effort paid off, and I made a significant impact on the policy success and outcomes for the communities affected by government decisions.
